An aside about the QA and dev mix for this particular position!


The combination aspect of this co-op position where QA and dev crossed paths in the form of automation was a lot more fun than I had expected!

It's not to say that I had low expectations, but rather, my expectations with regards to the meat and potatoes of the position were subverted.

The unique situation where I managed an automation related codebase that was not exposed to end users meant that I had a bit more leeway with regards to trying out new things, and this was really nice, as it resulted in several improvements to test code that came about from experimentation.

I can now definitely conclude that even though this position was not the straight development position that is highly coveted amoing co-op students, it was eye opening in several ways, and in the end, I coded just as much as my previous development related co-op position, if not even more!
